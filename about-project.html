About the project

This page explains the purpose of the hub, its current scope and the longer-term vision behind the framework.

Why the hub exists

The hub exists to make employment and settlement systems easier to understand, especially for people who already have valuable experience but find that systems, language and local assumptions make that value harder to see.

It also exists to help employers and communities make better use of international talent by reducing confusion, narrow screening and avoidable exclusion.

What the prototype does

This prototype brings together practical guidance on living, working and learning in the UK. It also includes a London-focused pathway tool designed to help users translate broad information into clearer next steps.

What the prototype does not do

It does not replace legal advice, regulated immigration advice, or formal casework. It does not guarantee eligibility, sponsorship or settlement outcomes. It is a guidance framework, not a decision-making authority.

Future vision

The framework is designed to be replicable and expandable. Over time, it could grow into a stronger resource for different sectors, different UK regions and other national contexts, while keeping the same focus on clarity, belonging and practical access.
