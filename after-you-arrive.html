After you arrive

The first days after arrival are often about small practical steps. Focus on identity, money, connectivity, transport and the services you may need soon.

Your first priorities

Once you arrive, focus on the basics first: where you are staying, how you will travel, how you will communicate, and what proof of identity or status you may need for work or renting.

Try not to solve everything in one day. A short checklist helps more than rushing through many systems at once.

Banking and money

You may need a UK bank account soon, especially for salary payments and rent. Requirements vary by provider, but many banks will ask for identity documents and proof of address.

If you do not yet have a fixed address, look at digital banks or ask what alternative proof they accept.

Register for services

If you are staying for more than a very short period, think about registering with a GP, arranging a UK mobile number, and understanding local transport in your area.

If you have the right to work, you may also need a National Insurance number or may receive one through your visa process depending on your route.

Local support and orientation

Look for local advice services, community groups, migrant support organisations, libraries and career services. These can help reduce isolation and make systems easier to understand.

Support is not only for crises. It can also help with confidence, belonging and practical problem-solving.

Get around confidently

Travel is often one of the first daily systems people need to learn. In London, contactless payment is usually the easiest place to start. Outside London, local bus and rail systems may work differently.

Go to transport for practical guidance.
