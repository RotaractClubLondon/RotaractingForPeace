Apprenticeships and early career routes

Not every route into work starts at the final role you want. Early-career and transition routes can help people bridge between past experience and a more stable long-term path.

Apprenticeships

In England, apprenticeships combine paid work with study. GOV.UK explains that apprentices must be 16 or over, living in England and not in full-time education when they start. Apprentices are employees and must be paid at least the minimum wage.

Graduate routes

Some people move from study into work through the Graduate route or through employer sponsorship. The exact route depends on immigration status, sector and how quickly a person can move into an eligible role.

Internships and placements

Internships can be useful, but not all unpaid internships are lawful. Rights depend on status and the real nature of the work. It is important to check whether the arrangement is legal and fair rather than assuming the title tells you everything.

Career-switch and entry-level alternatives

Sometimes the fastest route is not a direct continuation of your previous career. A support role, conversion route, temporary contract or adjacent occupation may build UK-relevant evidence while protecting your longer-term direction.
