Employers overview

This section is for employers who want to think more clearly about labour shortages, sponsorship, recruitment and retention. The aim is to make the system easier to understand and to show how international talent can help meet real workforce needs.

Why this matters

UK employers are still operating in a labour market with significant vacancy pressure. The Office for National Statistics estimated 721,000 vacancies in December 2025 to February 2026, and the 2024 Employer Skills Survey found that 27% of all vacancies were skill-shortage vacancies. This means the issue is often not only a lack of applicants, but a lack of applicants with the skills, qualifications or experience employers need.

Benefits of international talent

International hiring can help employers widen the talent pool, fill harder-to-fill roles and reduce the risk of relying on too narrow a search pattern. It can also strengthen language capability, market insight and international perspective in sectors that already operate across borders.

London workforce context

London's economy is especially strong in financial and business services, technology, life sciences, creative services, the visitor economy and the green economy. In sectors like these, employers often benefit when recruitment reflects the city’s global labour market rather than a narrower local assumption about where talent should come from.
