Employment support services

Good support does more than give information. It can help people understand what to aim for, how to present themselves, and how to keep confidence while moving through a new system.

Career support

The National Careers Service offers information, advice and guidance in England. This includes job profiles, careers advice and tools that can help people compare routes.

National Careers Service

Language support

Professional English matters in applications, interviews and day-to-day trust at work. Support may mean ESOL, sector-specific language help, or confidence-building around speaking in professional contexts.

Job-readiness help

This can include CV support, interview preparation, understanding UK job ads and identifying transferable skills. It is often most useful when it is tied to a real target sector.

Mentoring and coaching

Mentors can help people read the culture around a sector, not only the formal entry rules. This is often where belonging, confidence and practical strategy start to improve together.
