Find a job

Finding work in the UK usually takes more than one route. This page focuses on practical search methods, not just sending more applications.

Job search routes

Start with public vacancy platforms, employer websites and sector-specific recruiters. The government's Find a Job service is one of the main national vacancy platforms, while the National Careers Service offers careers advice and job profiles in England.

Job boards and recruiters

Recruiters are especially common in sectors such as finance, technology, construction and professional services. In other sectors, direct applications or local networks may matter more.

It helps to learn which search route is normal for your field instead of using the same method everywhere.

Networking

Networking does not only mean formal events. It can include LinkedIn conversations, alumni groups, volunteering, sector communities and introductions through people who already trust your work.

Many people find that informal visibility matters as much as the number of applications they send.

Targeting London opportunities

London is useful when your sector has strong demand there, but it should still be weighed against rent, travel and competition. For some people, beginning in another city may be more sustainable.

Try to compare opportunity with cost, not opportunity alone.

Entry routes for newcomers

People enter the market through different routes: graduate schemes, support roles, temporary work, apprenticeships, project contracts or direct sponsorship. The right route depends on whether you already have the right to work, whether your profession is regulated, and how transferable your experience is.
