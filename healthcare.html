Healthcare

Healthcare can feel confusing at first because there are different entry points for routine care, urgent advice and emergencies. This page gives a simple starting map.

How healthcare works

The UK has a public health system known as the NHS. Access depends on where you live, your immigration route and the type of care you need. The NHS works across the UK, but details differ by nation.

For migrants, GOV.UK's migrant health guide is a good starting point.

Registering for routine care

A GP practice is usually the first point of contact for non-emergency health needs. If you are settling in an area, register with a local GP as early as you can rather than waiting until you are unwell.

You do not normally need proof of address in exactly the same form everywhere, so ask the practice what they accept if you are new.

Urgent and non-urgent care

Use a GP for routine care and ongoing health needs. For urgent medical advice that is not an emergency, use NHS 111 online or by phone. This is the main urgent advice route in England and can help direct you to the right service.

If someone is seriously ill, badly injured, having trouble breathing, showing signs of stroke, or in immediate danger, call 999. You can also use 112, which works as an emergency number too.

Prepare for your first months

Bring details of regular medicines, vaccinations and important health history if you have them. If English is not your strongest language, ask whether interpretation support is available.

Good preparation does not remove all uncertainty, but it can reduce stress when help is needed quickly.
