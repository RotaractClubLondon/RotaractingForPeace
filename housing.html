Housing

Housing can shape whether arrival feels stable or stressful. This page explains the main rental basics, common terms and warning signs to know early.

Renting in the UK

Many new arrivals rent first. In England, landlords and agents often ask for identity documents, immigration status evidence, proof of income and a deposit.

GOV.UK's How to Rent guide is a useful starting point for England. Rules can differ in Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland.

Deposits and contracts

A tenancy agreement explains the main rental terms. Read it carefully before signing. In England, a tenancy deposit for most assured shorthold tenancies should usually be protected in a government-approved scheme.

If something feels unclear, slow down and ask questions before paying.

Common housing terms

Deposit is money paid upfront as security. Rent in advance is an early payment period. Bills included means some costs are included in rent, but not always all of them. Guarantor means a person who agrees to cover rent if you cannot.

Understanding the words helps you compare offers more confidently.

London-specific challenges

In London, price pressure and speed are major issues. Good properties can move quickly. Some people feel pushed into decisions before they have time to check details properly.

Try to balance urgency with caution. A bad housing decision can make every other part of settling harder.

Avoid scams

Be careful if someone refuses to show the property, pressures you to transfer money immediately, or avoids giving a clear written agreement. Do not rely only on screenshots or messaging-app promises.

Use known rental platforms where possible, ask for full addresses, and check who you are dealing with.
