Inclusive recruitment

Inclusive recruitment is not about lowering standards. It is about making sure standards are clear, relevant and not unintentionally built around assumptions that exclude strong candidates.

Reviewing job descriptions

Check whether every requirement is truly necessary. Some job descriptions mix essential skills with preferences, which can make a role look inaccessible even when the person could do it well with a short adjustment period.

Fairer screening

Fairer screening starts when employers look for evidence of capability, not only familiarity of background. A candidate may be strong even if their institution, title or country context is new to the panel.

Avoiding 'UK-only' assumptions

Some employers use language such as “UK experience required” when what they actually need is evidence of a certain type of responsibility, communication or system knowledge. Being precise about the real requirement can widen access without weakening quality.

Interview inclusion

Interviews work better when questions are clear, examples are welcomed from more than one system or country, and panellists are open to unfamiliar career routes. This helps employers assess substance rather than fluency in local norms alone.
