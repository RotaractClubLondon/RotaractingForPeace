Job search and applications

This page explains how to present experience clearly in the UK market. The goal is not to reduce your background. It is to make it easier for employers to understand it.

UK CV basics

UK CVs are usually concise and focused on relevance. Many employers want a clear summary, role-specific evidence and practical results instead of long descriptions of every duty you have ever done.

Cover letters and application forms

Cover letters are most useful when they explain fit, motivation and context in a very specific way. In the public and charity sectors, application forms can matter more than the CV and often ask you to address a person specification directly.

Interviews

Structured interviews are common. Employers often expect examples that show what you did, what happened, and what you learned. Good examples usually feel more convincing than general claims.

LinkedIn and professional visibility

LinkedIn can help employers understand your target clearly. It can also support lighter forms of networking before formal applications begin.

Translating international experience into UK language

This is one of the most important parts of the hub. The purpose is not to hide where you have worked. It is to translate your work into terms UK employers recognise more easily, including titles, systems, scope and outcomes.
