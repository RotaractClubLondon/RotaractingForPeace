Living in London

London offers access, diversity and opportunity, but it also comes with higher costs and faster competition. This page explains the main realities to plan for.

Why London feels different

London is the UK's biggest labour market and one of its most international environments. Many sectors are concentrated here, including finance, professional services, media, policy, culture and parts of tech.

It can be a strong place to build networks, but it also asks more of people financially and emotionally in the first stage.

Cost of living

Rent is usually the biggest difference between London and other UK cities. Daily transport, food near central areas and social costs can also rise quickly.

Many people manage costs by sharing housing, living further from central areas, or balancing travel time against rent.

Commuting and transport

London's transport network is extensive, but daily travel can still take time and money. Where you live shapes your routine, your social life and your budget.

Before choosing an area, look at the full commute, not only the rent. See transport guidance.

Housing realities

Competition for rental properties can be intense. Properties may move quickly, and some landlords or agents ask for several documents at once.

Check whether you can realistically afford an area before committing to it. See the housing page for terms, deposits and warning signs.

Boroughs and neighbourhoods

London is not one single experience. Different boroughs vary greatly by cost, transport links, pace, safety, services and community feel.

Try to think in terms of travel patterns and daily life, not just prestige or postcode.
