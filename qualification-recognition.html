Qualification recognition

Not every qualification problem needs formal recognition, but some do. This page helps separate academic understanding from professional licensing and shows when extra steps may matter.

Understanding overseas qualifications

Employers do not always know how to read qualifications from other countries. Sometimes they simply need a clear explanation. In other cases, a formal statement or regulated recognition process may be useful.

Academic versus professional recognition

Academic recognition is about how a qualification compares within the education system. Professional recognition is about whether you are allowed to practise in a regulated field. These are not the same thing, and people often need to know which question they are actually trying to answer.

When recognition is needed

Recognition is often most important for regulated professions such as healthcare, teaching and some technical fields. In non-regulated roles, explanation and translation may matter more than formal equivalence.

Useful preparation steps

Keep clear records of transcripts, certificates, course outlines and professional registration history if relevant. Even when formal recognition is not required, these records can make your profile easier to understand and trust.
