Regulated professions and accreditation

Some jobs can be entered through a normal application. Others need formal registration, licensing or recognition first. This page helps explain the difference.

What regulated professions are

There are over 160 professions regulated by legislation across the UK. In these professions, a regulator or recognised body controls who can practise, use a title or meet the standard for entry.

Why recognition matters

Recognition protects standards and public safety, but it can also create confusion for internationally trained professionals. It is important to know early whether your profession is regulated or whether your qualification mainly needs explanation rather than formal recognition.

Qualification checks, licensing and registration

The process depends on the profession. Health, teaching and some technical routes may need registration or a formal recognition process before full practice.

Recognition of professional qualifications policy statement

Regulated and non-regulated routes

Some people do not need to begin in the fully regulated role immediately. Depending on the field, there may be related jobs, support roles or adjacent pathways that allow you to build experience while you work through recognition steps.
