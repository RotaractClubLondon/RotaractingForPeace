Sectors in demand

This page uses broad sectors rather than a long technical occupation list. That makes it easier to understand where experience may fit and what kind of barriers or opportunities may come with each route.

Start with the sector, not only the title

A job title used in one country may sit inside a different team, level or function in the UK. Looking at the wider sector first can help you find a more realistic entry point.

How to use this page

For each sector, look at five things: what the sector covers, example roles, whether London is a strong market, whether sponsorship is common, and whether licensing or registration may be needed.

Health and Care

Overview: includes hospitals, social care, community health and support services.

Example roles: nurse, care worker, healthcare assistant, therapist, social care manager.

London relevance: high demand and many large employers.

Sponsorship: often possible in eligible roles with approved sponsors.

Regulation: many roles are regulated.

Digital and Tech

Overview: software, data, digital delivery, cyber and product roles.

Example roles: software developer, data analyst, product manager, UX researcher.

London relevance: strong, though not limited to London.

Sponsorship: possible in many firms, but competition is high.

Regulation: usually non-regulated.

Engineering and Construction

Overview: infrastructure, design, site delivery, maintenance and technical project work.

Example roles: civil engineer, design engineer, site manager, estimator, surveyor.

London relevance: strong because of transport, housing and commercial development.

Sponsorship: varies by role and employer.

Regulation: some routes need formal accreditation more than legal regulation.

Education

Overview: schools, colleges, training providers and education support.

Example roles: teacher, teaching assistant, lecturer, learning support worker.

London relevance: high demand, but cost of living matters.

Sponsorship: possible in some settings.

Regulation: some teaching routes require recognition or registration.

Finance, Professional Services, Hospitality and Creative Work

These sectors are also important, but they do not all work in the same way. Finance and professional services often value clear commercial language and polish. Hospitality may offer faster entry but sometimes weaker stability. Creative and media roles often depend heavily on networks, portfolio and reputation.

The best next step is often to identify where your background is strongest and what kind of proof that sector expects.
