Settlement pathways

This page is not legal advice. It is a plain-language overview to help people understand how some routes can connect to longer-term plans and where to check official rules.

A short explainer

Some people come to the UK for study, some for work, some to join family, and some through protection routes. Not every route leads to settlement in the same way, and some routes are temporary while others may lead to longer-term residence.

The key is to know what your own route allows rather than assuming all visas work the same way.

Temporary and longer-term routes

A temporary route may allow work, study or residence for a set period without automatically leading to permanent status. A longer-term route may create a path to indefinite leave to remain if the conditions are met over time.

Official rules change, so always check current guidance.

Study to work and work to settlement

Some people move from study into work routes. Others begin on a work route and later look at settlement options if they remain eligible. The detail depends on the route, the time spent in the UK and whether all conditions continue to be met.

This is why planning ahead matters, even if settlement is not your first goal.

Important caveat

This site is for accessible guidance and orientation. It does not replace legal advice, regulated immigration advice or official government instructions. If your situation is complex, get route-specific advice from a qualified source.
