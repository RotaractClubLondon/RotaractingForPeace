Sponsorship basics

Sponsorship can look complex from the outside. This page keeps the explanation high level so employers can understand when it may be relevant and where the formal guidance sits.

What sponsorship is

For worker routes such as Skilled Worker, sponsorship means an organisation holds a sponsor licence and is allowed to assign a certificate of sponsorship to an eligible worker for an eligible role. It is a formal compliance system, not just a letter of support.

High-level process

At a high level, the employer applies for a sponsor licence, puts internal compliance systems in place, and then uses the Sponsorship Management System to sponsor eligible workers where appropriate. Sponsor duties continue after the licence is granted.

When sponsorship may be relevant

Sponsorship may be relevant when employers have persistent difficulty filling eligible roles, when specialist skill needs are narrow, or when the labour market they need to recruit from is clearly international. It is not the right answer for every vacancy, but it can be a very useful option for the right roles.

Important disclaimer

This page is only an orientation guide. Sponsorship rules change and can be detailed. Employers should rely on official guidance, and where needed regulated advice, before making a decision.
