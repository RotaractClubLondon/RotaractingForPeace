From study to work

This page focuses on transition. The key question is not only what you are studying, but how that learning will connect to real work, real evidence and a realistic location strategy.

Transition planning

Plan the move into work before your course ends. It is easier to build direction gradually than to wait until the final weeks and then search in a rush. The best plans usually combine timing, rights to work, networking and role targeting.

Work experience during or after study

Placements, part-time roles, volunteering, project work and internships can all help build UK-relevant evidence. Not every role has to be perfect. Sometimes the value is in what it makes visible about your skills.

Graduate pathways

Some people move into graduate schemes, some into direct applications, and some into sponsored roles depending on their immigration route and sector. If your route has time limits or conditions, make sure your planning reflects them.

London-specific strategy

London can offer strong employer concentration and networking value, but it also creates more competition and more cost. A London strategy works best when the sector really needs that location and the financial reality has been thought through clearly.
