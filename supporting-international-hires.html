Supporting international hires

Recruitment is only one part of the journey. If employers want international hiring to work well, the first months matter. Support affects both performance and retention.

Onboarding

Strong onboarding should explain not only tasks but systems, expectations, reporting lines and where to ask questions. International hires often need both role induction and workplace orientation at the same time.

Workplace culture

What feels obvious to an existing team may be invisible to a new hire. This can include meeting styles, levels of directness, turn-taking, escalation routes and how feedback is normally given. Good teams explain these things instead of assuming they will be absorbed silently.

Practical adjustment support

Practical support might include help understanding internal systems, local transport, relocation signposting, clear written information or a named person for early questions. Small supports can make a large difference to confidence and speed of adjustment.

Retention considerations

Retention improves when hires feel their skills are understood, their progression is possible and their concerns can be raised without risk. International recruitment works best when employers plan for inclusion after the offer, not only before it.
