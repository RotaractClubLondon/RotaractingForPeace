Transport

Transport shapes time, cost and daily energy. Understanding it early can make work, housing and everyday life easier to manage.

Public transport basics

Transport systems vary across the UK. Bigger cities usually have stronger bus and rail networks. Smaller places may depend more on buses or private cars.

Always check local rules, fares and operators in the area where you will live.

London transport

In London, contactless bank cards or mobile payments are usually the simplest option for many journeys. Some people also use Oyster cards depending on their routine.

Transport for London explains visitor and payment options here: best ways to pay.

Rail and bus systems

National rail is useful for travel between cities and commuter routes. Buses are often cheaper but slower. In London, buses can be a practical way to cut costs if your journey allows more time.

Outside London, check whether local tickets, passes or student discounts are available.

Travel cards and payments

In some cases, travelcards or railcards can reduce costs. If you are eligible, linking a railcard to an Oyster card may help with some off-peak London travel.

The best payment method depends on age, travel pattern and location.

Commuting tips

When comparing areas to live, always look at the full journey to work, including walking time, delays and cost over a month. A cheaper rent can become less attractive if the commute is long and expensive.

Try to test a route digitally before you commit to housing if possible.
