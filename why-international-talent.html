Why hire international talent

Hiring internationally is not a gesture. For many employers, it is a practical workforce decision. This page focuses on the business case in plain language.

Skills gaps

The 2024 Employer Skills Survey found that 6% of employers had a skill-shortage vacancy and that 27% of all vacancies were skill-shortage vacancies. When a role is repeatedly hard to fill, expanding the search pool becomes a practical response rather than an optional extra.

Employer Skills Survey 2024

Diversity and innovation

International hiring can bring broader experience, different market knowledge and stronger understanding of multilingual or cross-border environments. In many teams, the value is not only technical capacity but the ability to approach problems from more than one frame of reference.

International perspectives

Employers serving global clients, diverse communities or international supply chains often benefit when teams include people who understand different regulatory, cultural or commercial environments. This can be especially relevant in London, where many sectors are outward-facing by default.

Long-term workforce value

International recruitment should not be seen only as a short-term vacancy response. Done well, it can support workforce resilience, improve retention in harder-to-fill functions, and help employers build a stronger long-term talent pipeline.
