Work rights and employment standards

Understanding rights at work is part of reducing precarity. A job offer matters, but the contract, pay, hours and status of that job matter too.

Employee rights and status

Rights can depend on whether you are classed as an employee, a worker or self-employed. That status affects things like holiday pay, dismissal rights and some forms of protection.

Pay and contracts

All employees have an employment contract. GOV.UK explains that a contract sets out employment conditions, rights, responsibilities and duties. Employers must also give employees and workers a written statement of particulars.

Employment contracts overviewWritten statement of particulars

Working hours and minimum wage

The National Minimum Wage applies to eligible workers even if they are not paid by the hour. It helps to check how your pay is calculated and whether the hours expected of you are realistic and lawful.

Basic protections and warning signs

Watch for situations where key terms are unclear, written information is missing, pay arrangements do not make sense, or an employer expects you to work outside the terms of your permission to work. If something feels unclear, slow down and check it.
